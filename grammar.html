1. Phonology (Sounds)

The Draconic language features deep, resonant vowels and clipped consonants.

Vowels

A: [ɑ] as in "father"
E: [ɛ] as in "bed"
I: [i] as in "machine"
O: [ɔ] as in "stone"
U: [u] as in "moon"
Y: [ɪ] as in "rhythm"

Consonants

K, G, S, Z, R (rolled), TH, SH, V, M, L, N, CH, P, T, D

Diphthongs

AI - high, EA - day, OI - boy, AU - out, OU - how

2. Morphology (Word Formation)

Draconic uses agglutination to build meaning from morphemes.

Common Morphemes

Prefixes: 
Raak- = "Dragon"
Vura- = "Rune"
Urth- = "Ancient"
Dra- = "Power"
Azgar- = "Magic" / "Strength"
Suffixes: 
-haz = possessive
-voth = noun
-rath = verb
-kor = plural
Infixes: 
-th- = transformation / change

3. Syntax (Sentence Structure)

Sentence structure follows Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) order.

Raak’haz kalth’vak ir’raakh. — "The dragon breathes fire."
Vura’kalth vrakkar azgar. — "The rune binds power."

Verb Formation

Vura-rath = "to create a rune"
Vura’kalth rathor. = "The rune creates fire."

4. Sentence Construction Examples

Vorn’az thran ez? — "We see you?"
Vorn goth Karnaz. — "I'm going to the village."

Question Words

Vek? - "What?"
Karn? - "Who?"
Goth? - "Where?"
Yrrn? - "When?"

5. Possessive and Plural Forms

Possession: -haz
Raak’haz = "Dragon’s"
Vura’kalth haz = "Rune’s flame"

Plurality: -kor
Raak-kor = "Dragons"
Zaroth’kor = "Dooms"

6. Tense and Aspect

-th- infix suggests transformation or past context
Vura-th-rath — "To create/transform a rune"
Vura’kalth thran — "The rune saw"

7. Word Building and Combining

Raak’thor = "Dragon’s rune"
Azgar’voth = "Power source"
Raak’gor = "Dragon’s strength"
Gor’velth = "Big strength"

8. Draconic Sentence Structure Practice

Ez goth Zal’nirrn. — "You go evening."
Vorn velth, zorak. — "I'm strong, friend."

9. Crash Course: Speaking Draconic

Pronunciation Rules

A = ah | E = eh | I = ee | O = oh | U = oo
R = rolled | ' = glottal stop | Double consonants = stressed

SVO Sentence Examples

Vorn zar veth. — I am good.
Ez vek marnak. — You want food.
Izz thran zal’nirrn. — They see evening.

Core Structures

Raak’haz — Dragon’s
Thran marnak — Look at the food

Practice Phrases

Veth’ra — Hello
Vek’ra thrak? — What is that?
Ez velth? — Are you strong?
Vorn vek raruoki. — I want music.
Zaroth’ra — Goodbye
