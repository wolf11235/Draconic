2. Morphology (Word Formation)

Draconic is agglutinative — words are formed by combining smaller meaningful units (morphemes).

Common Morphemes

-haz: Possessive
Raak’haz → “dragon’s”

-kor: Plural
Raak’kor → “dragons”

🔸 Order: Possessive + Plural
Raak’haz’kor → “the dragons’”

3. Syntax (Sentence Structure)

Default Word Order: Subject–Object–Verb (SOV)

Vorn Marn Drumak. → “I eat meat.”
Ez’kor Thran Zarnath. → “You (plural) see the parent.”

7. Possessives & Plurals

Possessive: Add -haz → Zarnath’haz → “parent’s”
Plural: Add -kor → Gralnak’kor → “weapons”

🔸 Order: -haz (possessive) comes before -kor (plural)

8. Compounding

Draconic favors compound words.

Basic Rules: Combine two or more stems. Use apostrophes (') for clarity. Read left to right.

TypeExampleMeaning
Noun + NounDrak’Azgar“dragon power”
Noun + AdjRaak’gor“dragon strength”
Adj + NounVelth’Thurnak“strong sword”
Noun + VerbVura’rathor“rune creation”

10. Ceremonial Name Structure

Format: [Title]’[Attribute]’[Deed]’[Lineage]’[Domain]

Ul’nor’Velth’Vrakkar’Raak’haz’Kulet
→ “Leader of Strength, Binder, of the Dragon’s Mountain”

Mirrak’Thranak’Rathor’Zarnath’Kalth
→ “Child of Joy, Forger, of the Parent’s Flame”
